Why do we want to manage it?

  • this is an idea from the larger software world
  • Large enterprises/Oracle
  • implement repeatable and dependable processes
  • disaster recovery
  • Technically DON'T need to "manage" anything
  • Probably more than 90% or more websites manage their configuration
  • This is mostly a new concept for CMS
  • Direct interaction with your configuration
  • Users: what you do is what they get, immediately
  • This sounds great, right?
  • in the real world, things are more complex...
  • Your site is growing, more users
  • You probably have a "client" (or a boss)
  • You probably have some sort of a team
  • This other model is very error prone
  • If you break anything, even during the simplest of changes it breaks for your users
  • Very good reason working right on a live site is a bad idea
  • from day one your site starts getting more complex
  • you are adding things, changing things, fixing things
  • more time = more complexity
  • your team wants it to be easy to make changes
  • your client wants to approve things before they go live
  • they don't want things to break
  • users expect stability
