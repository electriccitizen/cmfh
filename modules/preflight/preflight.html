Why do we want to manage it?

  • This is an ancient problem from the larger software world
  • How do we implement repeatable and dependable processes to make changes and updates?
  • Technically, you DON'T need to "manage" anything
  • In fact, it is safe to say that MOST websites in existence aren't "managing" their configuration
  • Most people just make changes right on a production site, or manually upload new copies of a database
  • Users: what you do is what they get, immediately
  • This idea of managing configuration is mostly a new concept for CMS
  • But it sounds great, right?
  • in the real world, things are more complex...
  • Your site is growing, more users
  • You probably have a "client" (or a boss)
  • You probably have some sort of a team
  • This other model is very error prone
  • If you break anything, even during the simplest of changes it breaks for your users
  • Very good reason working right on a live site is a bad idea
  • your team wants it to be easy to make changes
  • your client wants to approve things before they go live
  • they don't want things to break
  • they don't want downtime or content freezes
  • over time, these expectations tend to grow
  • and then there is complexity...
  • from day one, your site starts getting more complex
  • you are adding things, changing things, fixing things
  • more time = more complexity

Ok, so HOW do we do manage it?

  • we know what configuration is and why need to manage it
  • how it is typically managed?
  • historically, setting up a testing or staging server is step one
  • But this alone doesn't really solve anything
  • We still have no good way of deploying changes from environment to another

Typical approaches

  • Content freezes
  • Code freezes
  • Features
  • Exportables and custom modules
  • This is how most people on most content management systems handle these things...

Sounds great

but in fact this is very painful and difficult.
  • Features are great but being misused
  • Can be very slow and error prone to build and manage
  • Not everything can be Featurized
  • Conflicts, refusal to revert, overridden status on prod
  • Sometimes it felt like half of teams life was devoted to simply fighting with Features
  • So can we fix this?
  • Most of this address was focused on the difficult problems surrounding D7
  • Dries announced a number of new initiatives for D8 in hopes of making things easier and better
  • Managing configuration was identified as one of the top pain points and it was the first intiative
  • He named Greg Dunlap the CMI lead a few weeks later and by DrupalCon Denver in 2012 the first iteration had been committed to Drupal 8 core
