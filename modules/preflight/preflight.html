What is configuration?

  • Let's start at the beginning
  • Millinium Falcon vs Starship Enterprise anecdote
  • config is the very first thing you do
  • wordpress, joomla, drupal etc. it does not matter
  • it is almost ALWAYS stored in the "database"
  • in Drupal you are also "configuring" your site based on install profile

Configuration

  • Site name and email address
  • Content types and fields
  • Menu and Taxonomy settings
  • Blocks/Block Layouts
  • Permissions and roles
  • View modes and layouts
  • Views settings
  • Active modules/module settings
  • Everything in Structure and Configuration...
  • there are lot of examples
  • as we will see later it becomes blurry
"Configuration tells your site what to do, and how to act."
  • It is distinct from your content (usually)
  • It is distinct from your theme (usually)
  • It lives in your database (historically)
  • ...

Why do we need to manage it?

  • Configuration management in the real world
  • Large enterprises/Oracle
  • implement repeatable and dependable processes
  • disaster recovery
  • Technically DON'T need to "manage" anything
  • This is mostly a new concept for CMS
  • We saw this in the UI and even in the docs
  • Single developer
  • Website in the cloud
  • Direct interaction with your configuration
  • Users: what you do is what they get, immediately
  • This very error prone
  • If you break anything, even during the simplest of changes it breaks for your users
  • Very good reason this is a bad idea
  • in the real world, things are more complex...
  • Your site is growing, more users
  • You probably have a "client" (or a boss)
  • You probably have some sort of a team
  • MORE complexity and HIGHER expectations
  • Things just can't "break"
  • People need to approve things before letting users see them
  • this will continue to grow as your project grows
  • there has to be a way to "manage all of this...

but in fact it was very painful and difficult.
  • We have mostly solved lots of previously very hard problems
  • and we do it better than most other CMS
  • but something is still very wrong.
  • Features are very slow and error prone
  • config is 30% faster, easily
  • Not everything can be Featurized
  • Conflicts, refusal to revert, client changes
  • Sometimes resort to double entry/config
  • "Code freezes" or "content freezes"
